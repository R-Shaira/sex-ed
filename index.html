Youth in the Philippines have the right to age-appropriate and scientifically accurate information about sexual and reproductive health, as mandated by Republic Act 10354. Comprehensive Sexuality Education (CSE) is implemented through DepEd Order #31, series of 2018, aligning with the national policy to address the reproductive health needs of citizens, including providing CSE for the youth. The objective is to equip young people with knowledge and life skills for informed decision-making, promoting sexual health, and safeguarding their rights.

Comprehensive sex education has been shown to contribute to delaying the age at which young people initiate sexual activity, promoting informed decision-making and responsible behavior.

Sex education equips individuals with knowledge about contraception, safer sex practices, and reproductive health, thereby lowering the rates of unintended pregnancies and sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

Sex education often includes guidance on building respectful and consensual relationships, fostering communication skills, and addressing issues such as consent, boundaries, and mutual respect.

By providing accurate information about sexuality, body image, and self-esteem, sex education contributes to improved mental health, helping individuals develop a positive and realistic understanding of their bodies and relationships.
